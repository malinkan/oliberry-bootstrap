My Story

My career path took me in a completely different direction, when I discovered my talent for math and going to a technical school seemed more reasonable. And even though it was a fun time working in a software engineering field, full of incredible experiences and learnings, it always felt that there have been something always missing and I am not doing what I felt is right for me. After taking some time of work and doing some much needed soulsearching, I have decided to trust my inner calling and reconnect with a creative part of self.

I remember on one cold dreary winter morning in Toronto, I woke up and just thought to myself: its time...its time to follow your heart and answer your inner calling; one you have always had yet had misplaced somewhere in the attic of life. It's time to pursue your true purpose and reconnect with your creative self.

And so with that I quit my engineering job at one of the top media companies in Canada, packed my belongings and began writing the first page in a new chapter in my life.

Floristic Portfolio

Floristic design is both an art and a skill; the skill can be thought but takes many years to master; the art is connected to something deeper within yourself - something almost primal; a desire to express your essence and translate it into something real that has the ability to evoke those same emotions in the heart and mind of others - it’s almost like telepathic communication where you tell a story or embody an emotion without using a single word.

Over the years I have accumulated a wealth of knowledge in the area of floristic design and I’ve labored tirelessly to hone my skills. I’ve attended many workshops (both in person and online), volunteered as a floristic designer, taken courses in color theory, and spent countless hours refining various aspects influencing the quality of a flower arrangement. My hope is to be able to tell a small story or awaken a dormant emotion when you interact with my arrangements. It is much more than a visual display; appreciating a floral arrangement is an exercise involving many of your senses: sense of touch, smell, sight, as well as your sense of wonder, sense of wonder, sense of style, and sometimes even your sense of humor :)

I'm thrilled to share with you some of my work, organised by a category: arrangement forms; color palette; and an occasion (purpose). I have also included some of my more recent seasonal work [late summer 2016] and will continue to update the gallery with more arrangements as they are created
